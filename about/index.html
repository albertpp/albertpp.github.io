About

Hello! I’m Yuang Chen (陈宇昂), a junior student in Peking University, College of Urban and Environmental Sciences(CUES), majoring in Environmental Science. My current interests in research are quite varied and cross-disciplinary, ranging from regional pollutants transmission (which includes emission-inventory compilation, air modeling and scenario analysis) to Policy analysis of emission from certain industries like cement, fertilizer and municipal waste. Read more about my research in this page.

 

Feel free to contact me via: cya.pku@gmail.com or cya@pku.edu.cn

 

#Personal Life

I was born on 25 July 2000, in Jiangsu Province, China. My hometown was where Yangtze River meets Dong Sea, the longest river and the most important Sea in China, respectively. My childhood happy days of swimming in clean water were gone in 2006 due to substantial factories and tons of pollutions pouring into rivers. And then there was black smoke and people catching diseases like cancer. I’m not sure but I always think my childhood memories of my hometown has something to do with my choosing to read Environmental Science.

Currently I’m a junior student in Peking University. I totally understand that I’m not a genius (geniuses are studying Maths and Physics). And people who study Geography and Environmental Science (while I study them both) really need some “dullness” (I’m not saying my classmates are dull), because it takes so much time to result in a work that may not mean a lot. But with so much work added up, every one can make a contribution, and that’s my understanding of my major and also my way of life.

#Students Union

For the past three years, I’ve devoted large amount of time to services, especially in Students Union. I’m currently the president of students union in CUES, leading a 50-people team. We have host dance party that contains 1000+ people and hold Cultural Festivals that attract official media’s attention.

My understanding of service work is that maybe it doesn’t do much help to my research, if any, but I can find happiness in helping people. When we gather everybody around in Mid-autumn Festival to make Moon cakes, I can see those smiling faces even though this is a simple activity. This has something to do with what I want to study–I can gain happiness when I find my research results can really become policy suggestions that may influence thousands of people. That’s why I love them both, the research and the service work.
